Age Discrimination

Age discrimination involves treating someone unfairly based on their age. This can occur in workplaces, social settings, and even in access to services. Often, this form of discrimination affects both younger and older people, with older adults sometimes excluded from job opportunities and younger individuals dismissed as inexperienced.

Gender Discrimination

Gender discrimination refers to unequal treatment based on someone's gender, often affecting women and gender-diverse individuals. This can include unequal pay, limited opportunities, and biased expectations based on societal gender roles.

Racial Discrimination

Racial discrimination is the unfair treatment of individuals based on race or ethnicity. This often includes systemic issues such as lack of representation, inequitable resources, and prejudiced assumptions about individuals from certain backgrounds.

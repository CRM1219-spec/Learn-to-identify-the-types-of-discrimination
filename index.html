Types of Discrimination

Age: Prejudice against people based on their age, assuming they are too young or too old to be competent in certain areas.

Gender: Discrimination based on gender roles, often resulting in unfair treatment, especially towards women in certain professional settings.

Racial: Unfair treatment due to someone's race, often resulting in exclusion or harmful stereotypes.

Religion: Discrimination against individuals based on their religious beliefs or practices, sometimes leading to unfair treatment in public or professional spaces.

Sexual Orientation: Negative attitudes or treatment toward individuals based on their sexual orientation.

Disability or Illness: Discrimination based on someone's physical or mental condition, which often leads to limiting their opportunities unnecessarily.

Linguistic Discrimination: Treating someone unfairly because of their native language or accent, often suggesting that they are not as competent.

Ideological Discrimination: Negative treatment based on someone's political or social beliefs, often dismissing or discrediting their viewpoints.

Origin or Ethnicity: Prejudice against people because of their ethnic background or national origin, leading to assumptions or exclusion.

Marital Status: Judgments or differential treatment based on someone's marital status, like assuming single people are incomplete.

Appearance: Bias based on physical appearance, often affecting people differently based on body size, attractiveness, or other physical features.

Social Class: Prejudice based on economic or social standing, leading to exclusion or assumptions based on perceived wealth.

Locality: Discrimination based on where a person lives, often associating them with certain stereotypes based on their neighborhood.

Skin Color: Discrimination based on the tone of a person's skin, often tied to racial stereotypes and bias.
